Local search has become the cornerstone of discovery for businesses with physical locations. When potential customers search "coffee shop near me" or "plumber in Chicago," Google My Business (GMB) listings dominate the results. If your business isn't ranking well, you're leaving money on the table.

Here are five proven strategies to improve your GMB ranking and attract more local customers.

## 1. Complete Every Section of Your Profile

Google rewards completeness. A fully filled-out profile signals to Google that your business is legitimate and provides value to searchers.

### Essential Sections to Complete

**Business Information:**
- Accurate business name (as it appears in the real world)
- Complete address with suite numbers if applicable
- Local phone number (not a toll-free number)
- Website URL
- Business hours (including special holiday hours)

**Business Category:**
- Primary category that best describes your main service
- Secondary categories for additional services
- Be specific rather than broad

**Business Description:**
Write a compelling 750-character description that includes:
- What your business does
- What makes you unique
- Your service area
- Natural keywords (don't stuff!)

### Pro Tips for Profile Completion

- Add products and services with descriptions and prices
- Include attributes (wheelchair accessible, free Wi-Fi, etc.)
- Upload your logo and cover photo
- Add menu items if you're a restaurant

## 2. Generate and Respond to Reviews

Reviews are one of the most powerful ranking factors for local search. They signal trust to both Google and potential customers.

### How to Get More Reviews

**Ask at the right moment:**
- After a successful transaction
- When a customer expresses satisfaction
- Following project completion

**Make it easy:**
- Send a direct link to your review page
- Include QR codes on receipts or signage
- Use email follow-ups with review requests

**What NOT to do:**
- Never buy fake reviews
- Don't review-gate (only asking happy customers)
- Avoid incentivizing reviews with discounts

### Responding to Reviews

Every review deserves a response. Here's why it matters:

**For positive reviews:**
- Thank the customer by name
- Reference something specific from their experience
- Keep it genuine and personal

**For negative reviews:**
- Respond quickly (within 24 hours if possible)
- Apologize sincerely
- Take the conversation offline to resolve
- Follow up publicly when resolved

## 3. Optimize for Local Keywords

Your GMB listing and website should work together to signal relevance for local searches.

### On Your GMB Profile

Include location-relevant terms naturally in:
- Business description
- Product and service descriptions
- Posts and updates
- Photo captions and file names

### On Your Website

- Create location-specific landing pages
- Include city and neighborhood names in content
- Add local schema markup
- Build local citations on relevant directories

### Keyword Research for Local SEO

Use tools to discover:
- Search volume for local terms
- Related local keywords
- Question-based searches in your area
- Seasonal trends in local search

## 4. Post Regular Updates

Google My Business posts keep your profile active and provide fresh content for searchers to engage with.

### Types of GMB Posts

**What's New:**
- Business updates and news
- New products or services
- Team announcements

**Events:**
- Upcoming workshops or classes
- Community events you're hosting
- Sales or special occasions

**Offers:**
- Limited-time discounts
- Special promotions
- Seasonal deals

### Best Practices for GMB Posts

- Post at least once per week
- Use high-quality images (1200x900 pixels minimum)
- Include a clear call-to-action
- Keep text concise (150-300 characters)
- Use relevant keywords naturally

## 5. Build Local Citations

Citations are mentions of your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) on other websites. Consistent citations help Google verify your business information.

### Priority Citation Sources

**General Directories:**
- Yelp
- Yellow Pages
- Better Business Bureau
- Facebook Business

**Industry-Specific Directories:**
- TripAdvisor (hospitality)
- Healthgrades (healthcare)
- Avvo (legal)
- Houzz (home services)

**Local Directories:**
- Chamber of Commerce
- Local business associations
- City/community websites
- Local news site directories

### Maintaining Citation Consistency

Your NAP should be identical everywhere:
- Same business name format
- Same address format (St. vs Street)
- Same phone number format
- Same suite/unit notation

### Auditing Your Citations

Regularly check:
- Accuracy of existing citations
- Duplicate listings that need merging
- New opportunities for citations
- Competitor citation sources

## Tracking Your Progress

Improvement takes time. Monitor these metrics in your GMB dashboard:

**Visibility Metrics:**
- Search views (how often you appear)
- Maps views (map-specific appearances)
- Search queries (what people searched)

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Website clicks
- Direction requests
- Phone calls
- Photo views

**Compare over time:**
- Month-over-month trends
- Year-over-year growth
- Performance vs. competitors

## Quick Wins You Can Do Today

1. Log into your GMB profile and check for incomplete sections
2. Respond to any unanswered reviews
3. Create and publish a new post
4. Upload 3-5 new photos
5. Verify your hours are accurate
